### Format

- **Wellington Culinary Events Trust (WCET)** runs WOAP. Visa = naming rights sponsor since 2010.
- **Three audience layers:**
  1. Wellington locals (~200k metro pop) — the mass-market eaters.
  2. Domestic culinary tourists (Auckland, Christchurch, Hamilton, Tauranga foodies — about a third of attendees travel in for it).
  3. Trade & media — chefs, food writers, MasterChef-adjacent industry, Cuisine + Eat magazine, Stuff/RNZ food editors.
- **2026 is 18th edition.** Mature, well-trafficked, established protocols.

### Producer/supplier participation channel

WOAP industry contact: `industry@visawoap.com` (per visawoap.com/industry). Accepts:
- Eateries (host an event, run a Burger Wellington entry, run a Dine Wellington offering)
- Bars (cocktail/beverage events)
- Event hosts (long lunch, masterclass, paddock dinner, brewery tour, etc.)
- **Food & drink suppliers/producers** (this is Mangaroa's lane) — not a publicly listed program but the industry mailing list pushes "supplier opportunities" through the year per their copy

## 2. Mangaroa fit — KAUPAPA + positioning pass

### Where Mangaroa lines up well with WOAP

- **"Locally raised, locally processed" = the WOAP origin story.** WCET's founding pitch was Wellington-region producers + chefs winter-low-season collab. Mangaroa is geographically and ethically on-thesis (Wairarapa = WOAP region; closed-loop processing = WOAP supplier-of-the-year-style narrative).
- **Story already shoot-ready.** Cycle 4's `meat-launch-concept.md` has paddock→butchery→counter as the load-bearing visual — that IS the WOAP story.
- **Meat-launch sequence drops 2026-05-10.** Three months ahead of Burger Wellington — perfect runway for restaurant supplier conversations seeded off the launch (May = "we just opened our shop", June = "let's chat about your WOAP burger", July = supply confirmed, Aug = served).
- **Earth Fellows / regenerative axis** fits the chef-judge sensibility. Burger Wellington People's Choice goes to whatever the public votes; the Critics' Choice and Industry Awards reward provenance, story, technique. Restaurants paired with us would score on the provenance/story dimension.
- **KAUPAPA pass:** love (chefs sourcing with care for the animal), life (cattle on pasture, processed by hand 30km from where they're served), oneness (the closed loop made visible to the festival audience). Clean ✅.

### Where it doesn't (or doesn't yet)

- **No restaurant relationships built yet.** Mangaroa has the meat, the story, the volume — but no warm chef contacts in Wellington. Cold outreach in May/June to restaurants finalising their Burger Wellington concepts is doable but tight.
- **Volume question.** Burger Wellington restaurants need consistent supply across 3 weeks. Mangaroa's processing throughput at Preston's needs to be confirmed against a "X kg/week × Y restaurants for 3 weeks" load.
- **No festival-event format ready.** A "Mangaroa Closed Loop Dinner" at the farm or in-shop is a 2027 concept — not bolt-on for 2026.
- **Wellington-region is technically Wairarapa-included,** but check WCET's geographic boundary on supplier eligibility — Mangaroa's at the eastern edge of what most Wellingtonians count as "the region." Likely fine; flag for Natalie to confirm.

## 3. Three options on the table

### Option A — Skip 2026 entirely. Wait for 2027 application open. ❌ Too passive

- Pros: zero workload, zero risk.
- Cons: leaves the meat-launch momentum stranded, no 2026 trade exposure, no validation of restaurant supplier model before 2027 commits.

### Option B — Direct festival entry (host event, run Burger Wellington) ❌ Closed window

- Pros: maximum brand exposure, ticketed revenue.
- Cons: applications closed Feb 2026. A late-add request to industry@visawoap.com is a low-probability ask three months out; would need exceptional pitch and likely existing relationships. Not realistic for a first-year participant.

### Option C — B2B supplier route in 2026 + lock 2027 host slot ✅ Recommended

- **2026:** Reach out to 5-10 Wellington/Wairarapa restaurants that have historically run strong Burger Wellington entries. Pitch Mangaroa as their meat supplier with credit-on-burger ("Mangaroa Wairarapa beef") in their entry blurb. Trade pricing tier specifically for WOAP-supplied burgers.
- **2027:** Apply in early Feb 2027 to host a Mangaroa-led event. Concept TBD but obvious anchors: paddock-to-plate dinner at the farm, in-shop "from the butchery" tasting, masterclass on closed-loop sourcing.

Option C is the right shape because it lets us *test the supplier relationships in 2026* (small risk, high learning) before committing to a hosted event in 2027 (bigger swing, needs the chef relationships already warm).

## 5. Failure modes

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| No restaurants bite in May/June outreach | Acceptable — 2026 was always the test year. Pivot to 2027 application with whatever supplier relationships did form. Don't force-fit. |
| WCET applies a strict Wellington-metro boundary that excludes Wairarapa suppliers | Confirm with industry@visawoap.com on first outreach. If excluded, the play becomes 2027 host-event only (skip the supplier track). |
| Processing capacity at Preston's can't handle 3-week supply load + regular shop demand | Stage outreach: confirm Preston's window first, then pitch only the volume we can actually deliver. Prefer 2-3 restaurants supplied well over 8 supplied half-heartedly. |
| Restaurant pitches Mangaroa to compete with their existing supplier (relationship damage) | Lead pitches with "we're the new processor in town, opening 2026-05-10, we'd like you to consider us alongside what you're already doing" — collaborative not competitive. |
| Burger Wellington competition pricing pressure (cheap chuck wins on margin) | Don't compete on $/kg with industrial. Compete on story, provenance, and the "locally processed" badge — chefs running provenance-led entries are NOT optimising for margin. |
| Late-add festival-event request bypasses the closed window | Possible but low-probability and requires existing chef-judge introduction. Not pursuing this cycle. If a relationship surfaces organically in May/June outreach, revisit. |
