# Locally Processed Meat — Messaging Pillars

## Core Line
**Home-grown, home-processed.**

## Supporting Line
From our paddocks to your plate — raised on regenerative pastures, processed by our local crew.

## Alternative Lines
- Grown in harmony with nature — raised right here, processed right here
- From the pasture to the butchery to your kitchen — all within the valley

---

## Pillar 1: Home-Processed
Our team, our craft, right here in the valley.

**Proof points:**
- Our animals are professionally slaughtered, then brought home for Rob and the crew to butcher and prepare
- Every cut is handled by people who know the farm, the animals, and the land
- Shorter supply chain means fresher meat and more care at every step
- Rob manages both the farm and the butchery — the same person who raises the animal prepares the cuts

**Key phrases:** home-processed, our local crew, right here in the valley, from our paddocks

---

## Pillar 2: Regenerative Provenance
The land, the farming, the full story you can trust.

**Proof points:**
- Pasture-raised beef and lamb grown in harmony with nature in Mangaroa Valley
- Virtual fencing and cover crops — innovative regenerative tools that produce more with fewer inputs
- Holter cattle as part of the regenerative herd — a breed suited to the land and the system
- You can trace every cut back to this farm — paddock, animal, crew
- The way we farm improves the soil, water, and biodiversity on the land

**Key phrases:** regenerative pastures, grown in harmony with nature, paddock to plate, traceable, Mangaroa Valley, virtual fencing

---

## Pillar 3: Quality and Trust
The same high standards, now with even more local care.

**Proof points:**
- Standard retail cuts: steaks, mince, sausages, chops, roasts — done well
- Beef and lamb raised with care, processed with skill
- Available in the shop and for pickup — reliable, consistent, local
- Staged approach to supply: starting with what we have, building as demand grows. Honest about availability

**Key phrases:** high standards, local care, done well, available now

---

## Pillar 4: Clear and Practical
What is available, how to get it, no fuss.

**Proof points:**
- Fresh meat in the shop — come in and choose your cuts
- Order online for pickup when it suits you
- Interested in a whole lamb or side of beef? Register your interest

**Key phrases:** fresh in the shop, order online, pickup, register interest

---

## Preston's Framing (IMPORTANT)
Preston's is a valued part of the process. They handle the professional slaughter. We handle the butchery and processing from there. This is not a replacement — it is a natural evolution of bringing more of the process home.

**Never say:**
- Anything implying Preston's was poor quality
- "We used to outsource" or "we've taken back control"
- Any comparison that positions old vs new

**Always say:**
- "Professionally slaughtered, then brought home for our crew to process"
- "A trusted part of the chain"
- Frame as additive, not corrective

## Heritage / Butchery History Story
Available as a content angle but **requires family approval before use**. All content must work without this story. If approved, it adds a layer of local history and continuity to the craft.

---

## Content Angles from Meeting (for social/newsletter/video)

1. **Rob's butchery story** — the person who raises the animals also does the butchery. Personal, hands-on, authentic.
2. **Virtual fencing / Holter cattle** — innovative regenerative tech, visual content (cattle moving with virtual fences, no physical fences). Good for video and farmer-audience content.
3. **Cover crops and soil health** — the farming system behind the meat. Shows what "regenerative" actually means in practice.
4. **TB testing and cattle movement** — behind-the-scenes farm ops. Upcoming filming opportunity for raw, real content.
5. **Staged supply approach** — honest about building this gradually. "We start with what we have and grow with demand." Builds trust.
6. **Whole/half animal orders** — a way for customers to buy into the full farm story, share with friends, fill the freezer.

## Tone
- Grounded, not hype
- Local, not corporate
- Confident, not defensive
- Practical, not precious
- No exclamation marks in brand copy
- No emojis in professional content
- NZ/Kiwi register: casual, humble, real
