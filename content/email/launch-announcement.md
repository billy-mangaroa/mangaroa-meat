Kia ora {{ first_name|default:'friend' }},

Hōtoke is the butchery season. The grass slows, the cattle have had a long summer on pasture, the lambs are ready, and the sheds quieten down enough for slow, careful work.

For the last two years, the meat we raised here at Mangaroa had to leave the farm to be cut and packed somewhere else. We've never been quite at peace with that — the animals are raised in alignment with this whenua, and it always felt like the cuts should come back home with the same care.

That changes this week.

Our on-farm butchery is open, and Rob has been in there laying down the first proper cuts of the season. Locally raised, locally processed — the whole story stays at Mangaroa now. Less travel, less handling, more mauri kept intact from paddock to plate.

What you'll find this week: variable-weight cuts of regeneratively raised Angus beef and Coopworth lamb, grass-fed, no synthetic hormones, no antibiotics, no GMOs. Three-year-old beef for flavour and marbling. Mince, sausages, sirloin, ribeye, lamb racks, rumps, the whole cabinet. Some boxes too, if you want a fortnightly habit rather than a weekly visit.

## Notes on voice & structure (for Grady's review)

- **Subject A** is the strongest because it states the differentiation in three beats. No clickbait, no urgency, sentence case — matches the Mangaroa newsletter pattern (e.g. "Autumn Newsletter (March)", "February Newsletter: Harvest Festival").
- **Opens with hōtoke / butchery season**, not with "We're excited to announce" or any banned opener. The seasonal frame is doing the work — early May in NZ is the historic time for stock to be processed. This roots the launch in the land's rhythm rather than in a marketing calendar.
- **Names Rob** — the brand voice doc is explicit that real people make real stories. Doesn't name surnames or get personal beyond what's needed.
- **"Locally raised, locally processed"** is the load-bearing phrase. Same construction as "transitioning dairy & pine into regenerative agriculture & native forests" — three beats, parallel structure, the brand's signature rhythm.
- **"Mauri kept intact from paddock to plate"** uses te reo naturally without translating. The reader either knows what mauri is or feels the meaning from context. Same with hōtoke and whenua.
- **No banned adjectives** (beautiful, amazing, gorgeous, etc.). No banned openers. No corporate jargon.
- **Two CTAs** — online + in-shop. Order is online-first because the meat.mangaroa.org infrastructure is what shipped this week, but the shop is honoured equally because that's the brand.
- **Discount-code hook is optional** — the auto-apply infrastructure shipped this run, so a launch-week code (e.g. `LOCAL10`) appended as `meat.mangaroa.org/discount/LOCAL10` will drop into Shopify checkout automatically. Currently NOT in the draft. Add only if Billy wants a launch promo and the code exists in Shopify Discounts.
- **Sign-off is brand-canonical** — "Ngā mihi aroha, The team at Mangaroa Farms".
- **Length** — variant A sits around 280 words in body, well within Mangaroa newsletter range (typically 250-450 words for the seasonal reflection + announcement type).
- **Variant B is the day-2 follow-up** — same audience minus openers. Short, punchy, no preamble, same CTAs. Standard re-engagement pattern.
