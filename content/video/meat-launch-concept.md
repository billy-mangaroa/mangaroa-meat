## 1 · The anchor (the one idea the film must carry)

> **Three places. Same hands. Same valley.**

The film is structured around three locations that are within a 2km drive of each other on the farm:
1. **Pasture** — animals at rest in the paddock, late afternoon golden hour preferred.
2. **Butchery** — the new on-farm room, Rob at the block, paper and steel and good light.
3. **Counter** — the shop cabinet, full, glass clean, a customer's hand pointing at a cut (with permission).

The hero film moves from one to the next without explanation. The viewer figures out the through-line themselves. **No voice-over needed for the hero.** The single load-bearing line — "**Locally raised. Locally processed.**" — appears once, on screen, near the end. That's the entire copy job.

**Variations to test as social hook lines** (for the 15s reel + caption layer):
- "From paddock to counter, all in the valley."
- "Two years off the farm. Now the cuts come home."
- "Same hands, paddock to plate."
- "The chain just got shorter."
- "Locally raised. Locally processed. Mauri kept intact."

**Banned framings** (do NOT shoot or edit toward these — they break the warmth dial in messaging-pillars.md):
- ❌ Anything implying Preston's was poor quality. Preston's is named in the email + on-the-land article as a trusted part of the chain — the camera does not visit Preston's, but copy/captions never position "in-house" as superior. Frame as additive, not corrective. (See messaging-pillars.md "Preston's Framing".)
- ❌ Slow-mo blood, sinew, or actively bloody butchery footage. Lawful, professional, but wrong tonal register for this piece. Bones, sides, and wrapped cuts on paper are fine.
- ❌ Drone footage that feels like a real-estate ad. If drone is used at all, keep it slow, low, and singular — one establishing pull-up over the paddock at dusk, not five.
- ❌ Shaky-cam "authenticity" affectations. Tripod and shoulder rig with intent — not Vimeo-staff-pick-grade chaos.
- ❌ Music swell on the cabinet reveal. The reveal is *done* by the cut and the colour, not by a string section.
- ❌ "Family business" close-up of multi-generation hands stacking on top of each other. Banned-warmth-cliché.
- ❌ Talent talking to camera about how meaningful this is. Rob talks to himself, to the lambs, to the knife. Not to the lens.

## 2 · Narrative arc — three-act (60–90s hero)

The hero film is structured as a single loop. Total target runtime: **75 seconds** (acceptable range 60–95s).

| Beat | Time | Place | What happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Open: pasture at rest | 0:00–0:18 | Paddock | Wide of cattle on hillside, late afternoon golden light. One animal turns its head. Cut to: lambs at the far fence, one mid-bite. Hold long enough that the viewer settles in. **Sound:** room — wind, distant bird, a single low call from a cow. |
| 2 — Move: the path home | 0:18–0:25 | Track / road | The transition. Tight shot of feet walking on a farm track, gravel crunch. Or: hands opening the butchery door from outside, daylight behind. Or: a side of beef being moved on a hook through a doorway, no faces. **Sound:** crunch, door, room shift. |
| 3 — Work: the butchery | 0:25–0:55 | Butchery | Rob at the block. Three to four shots, all hands-and-tools focus: knife sharpening on steel, paper laid down, a clean cut along a primal, mince emerging from the grinder, a wrap-and-tape on a tray. **Quiet-operators rule applies — Rob is at work, not performing.** The room is well-lit, paper everywhere, scales in shot. **Sound:** knife, paper rustle, scale beep, wrapping tape. No music yet. |
| 4 — Land: the counter | 0:55–1:08 | Shop | Cabinet wide — full, glass clean, evening. Tight on a chalkboard with the day's cuts. A hand (customer, with permission) reaches in and taps a cut. Cut to: Rob behind the cabinet, brief eye contact and a small nod — not a smile-for-camera. **Sound:** shop ambience, a quiet "thanks Rob" from off-camera, faint chiller hum. |
| 5 — Hold + line | 1:08–1:15 | Wide farm | Final wide — farm at dusk, cabinet light on in the foreground, paddock dark beyond. **Title card overlay**: *Locally raised. Locally processed.* Hold 3 beats. **Optional second card**: *meat.mangaroa.org · 98 Whitemans Valley Rd, Upper Hutt.* Fade. |

**No music**, or if music is essential to a Reel cut, use a single sustained tone (cello drone, low string pad) underneath beats 3–4 only, fading into room sound for beat 5. Do not use library "uplifting acoustic farm" tracks. Test with **no music first** — most viewers will read it as more honest.

**Voice-over: none** in the hero. The 30s recipe cut (§5.3) carries one line of Rob VO at the end, and the 15s reel hook (§5.1) uses on-screen captions only.

## 3 · Shot list — capture priorities for Miki

This is the brief Miki shoots to. Group by location for a single capture day; total target ≈ **6 hours on farm** to over-capture (we'll edit down). All shots are listed in priority order within their group — if the day runs short, drop from the bottom.

### A · Paddock (golden hour, ~30 min before sunset)

1. **Wide on cattle** — herd grazing on hillside, telephoto compression, sun behind camera. Hold 8s of stillness. *Critical for beat 1.*
2. **Single animal turning** — eyeline shot, telephoto, animal looks toward camera and away. *Critical.*
3. **Lambs at fence** — tight on grazing motion, mouths working. 3–4 takes.
4. **Pasture grass** — top-down, hand brushing through cover crop / pasture height. *Reference for sister farm-story film too.*
5. **Halter collar in close-up** — on a calm animal, frame the collar against the side of the head with the valley behind. *Reference for sister farm-story film.*
6. **Establisher: farm wide at dusk** — the closing beat. Tripod, neutral density filter if needed. Hold 15s.
7. **Optional drone** — single slow pull-up from the paddock to reveal the valley. ONE take, considered. Skip if the day is overcast.

### B · Track / between locations

8. **Feet on gravel** — POV-low or tight third-person, walking from paddock toward butchery direction. 2–3 takes for transition options.
9. **Butchery door from outside** — hand opening the door, daylight behind, dust motes if possible.
10. **Side on a hook through a doorway** — silhouette move, no faces. *Beat-2 transition option.*

### C · Butchery (best done in normal working session, not a "for camera" session)

> **Rob does what Rob would do anyway.** Miki shoots around him. No directions like "do that again, slower." Three rolls, hands-only framing, then we have plenty.

11. **Knife sharpening on steel** — tight, side-light, hands and steel only. *Critical.*
12. **Paper laid on block** — top-down, the moment a fresh sheet of butcher paper is rolled out. *Beat-3 opener.*
13. **Cut along a primal** — side view, hands-and-tool only, no chest-up framing of Rob. *Critical.* If a particular cut is too gory, swap for a clean cross-section reveal.
14. **Mince from grinder** — tight on the meat emerging onto paper. *Critical.*
15. **Wrap and tape** — top-down, wrapping a cut in paper, scale beeping. *Beat-3 closer.*
16. **Sharpie on the paper** — tight on the cut name being written: "lamb shoulder", "scotch fillet", etc. *Critical for chalkboard echo.*
17. **Rob at the block, wide** — one moment of him present in the room, half-frame, half-attention on the work. *Once is enough. Don't pose.*

### D · Shop counter (early morning before open OR after close — empty shop)

18. **Cabinet full, wide** — glass clean, cuts arranged. *Critical for beat 4.*
19. **Cabinet medium** — eye level, focus on the chalkboard with cut list. *Critical.*
20. **Customer hand pointing** — staged after-hours with a willing volunteer (see §6 for talent / consent). Camera behind/over shoulder, hand pointing at a cut.
21. **Rob behind the cabinet** — small nod, brief eye contact with off-camera "customer" (Miki's voice), then back to wrapping. **No smile-to-camera.** *Critical.*
22. **Chalkboard close-up** — paddock-of-origin written on the chalkboard ("Angus from the south paddock"). This anchors the Week +4 commitment in the launch calendar.

### E · Atmosphere (capture across the day, fold into transitions)

23. **Room sound rolls** — minimum 60s of clean ambient at each location (paddock wind, butchery quiet, shop ambient). *Critical for the no-music edit.*
24. **Hands washing** — tight, water and soap, between cuts. Useful B-roll.
25. **Sun through butchery window** — anything with light that grounds the day's time of day.

## 4 · Talent direction

### Rob

- **Voice in the film:** none in the hero. One line in the 30s recipe cut (§5.3): *"Three minutes a side. Rest five. Hot pan, salt, pepper. The animal did the slow work."* — recorded ambient at the block, not in a booth. Multiple takes, pick the most muttered one.
- **On camera:** at work. The lens is over Miki's shoulder — Rob is talking to the meat, the knife, or quietly to himself, not to camera. **One** moment of brief eye-contact at the cabinet (a nod, not a smile). That's it.
- **Wardrobe:** working butcher's apron over normal work clothes. Not a styled outfit. Not the Mangaroa-branded merch. The point is "this is what Rob is wearing today."
- **Permission:** confirmed at task creation — Rob is on board with being in the meat-launch comms. Re-confirm before each capture day; he can pull any specific shot. If he says "not that one," the take is cut. Non-negotiable.

### Murray, Zeb, Miki (other named people)

- **Quiet-operators rule** (per launch-content-calendar.md L24): named in copy, photographed at work not posed. Murray and Zeb may appear in B-roll if they happen to be in the paddock or shed during capture. **No staged "team shots."** No lineup. No portrait session.
- If Miki herself is needed in a frame (e.g. as the off-camera customer for the cabinet shot in §3D), her hand only — no face.

### Customers

- The cabinet customer-hand shot (#20) needs ONE willing volunteer. **Recruited explicitly off-camera before the shoot:** "Would you be okay with us filming your hand pointing at a cut? Just the hand, not your face — for our launch film." Clear consent, plain language, no model release theatre. Anyone uncomfortable, skip. The shot can also be a Mangaroa staff hand if no customer is around.
- Any face in frame from a customer requires the same explicit consent — and unless someone's whole face is critical to a beat (it isn't), default to not shooting faces. Hands, backs, shoulders, the cabinet glass reflection — all fine.

### Animals

- The animals don't perform. They're filmed in their normal grazing rhythm. **No pushing, calling, or staging cattle for camera.** If a calmer angle requires a slower approach, Miki uses telephoto and waits. The film must read as "the animal is at peace." Anything that breaks that reads to the audience as instinctive distrust.

## 8 · Failure modes — what to watch for in the cut

| Failure | What it looks like | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Reads as a tourism ad | Drone-heavy, stylised colour grade, swelling music, sunset every shot | Stick to room sound. ND filter optional but no over-saturation. ONE drone take or none. |
| Reads as patronising "small farm" pastiche | Too many warm filters, shallow DOF on every shot, "rustic" wood-and-twine props | Documentary lens choices. f/4–5.6 in butchery for context. No twine on cuts. |
| Rob looks performative | Eye contact with camera, smile-to-lens, "say it again" reshoot energy | Camera over Miki's shoulder, never head-on. One nod, no smile. If a take feels staged — discard. |
| Dignity break on the animal | Pushing/calling cattle for camera, distressed eyes, jerky framing of livestock | Telephoto, slow approach, wait for the moment. If the animal looks startled, that take is unusable. |
| Preston's framing breach | Any voice-over, caption, or B-roll that implies Preston's was poor or that we "took it back" | No copy in the hero film says anything about prior arrangements. The 30s recipe cut also does not. The narrative starts in the present — the film is about *now*, not about the contrast with the past. |
| Customer-face consent breach | Any identifiable customer face in the cut without explicit recorded consent | Default to hands/backs/silhouettes. If a face is in frame, get verbal consent on camera or cut the shot. |
| Captions read like ad copy | "Discover authentic, sustainable beef" / "Experience the Mangaroa difference" | The only captions in-frame are the on-screen text in §5.1 reel and the closing title card. No discovery, experience, journey, story, sustainable, authentic. |
| Music overrides the room | Library acoustic-folk track behind the entire film | No-music master cut as default. Tonal pad permitted only under beats 3–4 of the hero. Test silence first. |
| Reel hook misses in 1.5 seconds | First frame is a logo or a title card; no movement | Open the 15s reel on the pasture wide with motion (animal turning). Title card at the END only. |
