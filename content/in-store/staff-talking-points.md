# Staff Talking Points — Fresh Meat Launch

One-pager for shop staff. Keep by the counter. Use as a reference for customer conversations.

---

## The Key Message
"Our beef and lamb are raised right here on the farm and processed locally by Rob and the crew. Home-grown, home-processed."

## What Changed
- Rob and the crew now handle the butchery and processing on farm
- The animals are still professionally slaughtered by Preston's (that has not changed)
- We have brought the rest of the process home — cutting, processing, packing
- The same people who raise the animals do the butchery — that is the difference

## What is Available
- Standard retail cuts: steaks, mince, sausages, chops, roasts
- Beef and lamb
- Range changes week to week depending on processing schedule
- Both fresh and frozen options

## How Customers Can Buy
- **In store** — from the cabinet, as usual
- **Online** — meat.mangaroa.org/order, pickup from the shop
- **Bulk** — whole lamb, side of beef, custom orders. Point them to the form at meat.mangaroa.org/bulk or take their details

## If Someone Asks About Preston's
- "Preston's still does the slaughter for us — they are a trusted part of the process"
- "We have brought the butchery and processing side home"
- Do NOT say anything negative about Preston's or suggest previous processing was lower quality
- Frame it as: "We have added to the process, not replaced anything"

## If Someone Asks About the Farming
- "Our animals are pasture-raised on regenerative land right here in Mangaroa Valley"
- "We use virtual fencing and cover crops — farming that produces more with fewer inputs"
- "Regenerative means we farm in a way that builds soil health and supports biodiversity"
- "You can visit the farm and see the paddocks yourself"

## Bulk Orders — What to Say
- "We take expressions of interest for whole lamb, side of beef, and custom bulk orders"
- "A whole lamb butchered starts from about $350-450. Beef pricing depends on weight"
- "You can get it butchered into standard cuts, or pick up the carcass and do it yourself"
- "Fill out the form online or leave your details with us and we will be in touch"
- "Timing depends on the processing schedule — we will work it out with you"

## If You Do Not Know the Answer
- "Let me check with the team and get back to you"
- Take their name and phone/email
- Do not guess or make promises about products that are not confirmed

## Storage and shelf-life (quick answers)
- Fresh cuts: "Keep it in the fridge at 4°C or below, use within 3-5 days"
- Mince and sausages: "Best within 2 days fresh, or freeze on the day you collect"
- Freezing: "Well-wrapped, it keeps for months. Defrost slowly in the fridge — not on the bench"
- If asked "is it OK to freeze it now": "Yes, freeze on the day of pickup for best quality"

## Sausage and allergen questions
- "Recipes vary by batch — the ingredients are on the label in the cabinet"
- "If you have a specific allergy, tell me and I will check what is in this batch"
- Do NOT improvise on gluten-free/dairy-free claims. Read the label or check with the team.

## "Why is it more than the supermarket?"
- Stay grounded, not defensive
- "Supermarket meat is a different product — different farming, different processing chain, different scale"
- "What you are paying for is the animals being raised here, on regenerative pastures, and our crew handling the butchery"
- "It is meat with a paddock you can walk on and a butcher you can meet"
- Do not run down the supermarket. Just be clear about what is different.

## "Why isn't [specific cut] available today?"
- Be honest, tie to staged-supply messaging
- "The range changes week to week depending on what we have processed"
- "We are starting with what we have and growing with demand — we would rather be honest about availability than over-promise"
- Offer the alternative: "We have [X] today which works similarly", or take their name for next batch

## "Can I visit the farm? / Can I meet Rob?"
- Yes, encouraged. "We are at 108 Whitemans Valley Road, Upper Hutt — same as the shop"
- Rob is around most days but not always at the counter — "Drop in, ask for him, we will see if he is free"
- For a proper farm tour, take their details — Billy or the team will arrange

## "Is it organic? Spray-free?"
- Don't claim certifications we do not hold
- "We farm regeneratively — pasture-raised, no feedlot, virtual fencing, cover crops"
- "We are not certified organic, but we farm in a way that builds soil and supports biodiversity"
- If they want detail: take their name, point to the farming page or hand them off to Billy

## Capture for the email list / bulk interest
- Always offer: "Want me to put you on the list? You will hear when fresh stock comes in"
- Bulk interest: "Take a flyer or fill out this card — we will be in touch about whole lamb or side of beef"
- If they decline, no pressure. Smile, thank them, move on.

## Frozen vs fresh — when to recommend which
- "Fresh is great if you are cooking in the next few days"
- "Frozen is just as good for the freezer and lets you stock up"
- "Both come from the same animals, processed by the same crew"
