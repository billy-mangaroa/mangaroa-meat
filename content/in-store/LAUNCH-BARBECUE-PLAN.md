## 1. Concept & vibe

**One line:** "Come and meet the butcher, taste a few cuts, look around the cabinet, ask anything."

**Tone:** Same register as the rest of the launch — quiet, generous, place-grounded, no marketing voice. Rob's at the BBQ (or near it). Billy or Helena floats. A handful of farm team are around to answer questions. The cabinet is open and stocked. Music low or none. People eat, browse, leave. Some will buy a cut on the spot. Some will sign up for a bulk order. Some will just say hello and head home.

**What this is NOT:**
- A formal opening with speeches (those don't land in our register)
- A ticketed or RSVP'd event (low friction, walk-in)
- A commercial sausage sizzle (this is a tasting of the actual cuts, not generic sausages)
- A children's day (kids are welcome but the focus is the meat and the people behind it)

**Load-bearing phrase across signage, social, and on-the-day chat:**
> **Locally raised. Locally processed.**

## 3. Sampling format — what to cook, how much

### What to cook (tasting-portion cuts)

Goal: people taste 2–3 cuts in small portions, understand the cabinet without filling up. Aim for ~120 servings total (assumes 60–80 attendees, some second-helpings).

| Cut | Why this one | Portion | Total raw needed | Cook method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beef sausages (Rob's mix, if available; otherwise breakfast sausage) | Familiar, easy to grab, kids' option | 1 mini sausage / 30g | ~3.6 kg | BBQ direct heat |
| Lamb shoulder slices (slow-cooked the night before, finished on grill) | Showcases low-and-slow cut most people don't cook themselves | 25g slice on a small slice of bread | ~3 kg cooked weight (so ~4.5 kg raw shoulder) | Sous-vide or 6-hr 130°C oven night before; finish on BBQ |
| Beef sirloin or rump cut into ~2cm strips | The hero — premium pasture-fed beef tasted on its own merits | 25g strip, no sauce | ~3 kg | BBQ direct heat, rest 3 min before slicing |

Optional addition if budget/staff allows:
| Lamb merguez or chorizo (if Rob has any in stock) | Shows charcuterie ambition, lifts the table beyond "just meat" | half-link / 20g | 1.5 kg |

**Seasoning:** salt + cracked pepper only on the BBQ cuts. The lamb shoulder gets its own slow-cook seasoning the night before (Rob's call — rosemary + garlic + olive oil works). No marinades on the day. The point is to taste **the meat**, not the chef.

**Bread:** order or buy in 4–5 baguettes from a local baker (e.g. Pandoro or any UH baker who can do morning-of) for the lamb-shoulder slider. Cut into 30g slices. Don't make formal sliders with toppings — let the meat carry it.

**Sides (optional, not load-bearing):**
- A bowl of pickled veg or chutney from the farm garden / a Mangaroa supplier — if Helena has this on hand
- One bowl of in-shell hazelnuts or similar — gives people something to do with their hands while they wait

**Drinks:**
- Big jug of water with lemon
- Big jug of cordial (Six Barrel Soda or local equivalent if there's a relationship)
- Filter coffee in an urn — separate table, away from BBQ smoke

**No alcohol.** Daytime, family-friendly, and removes the licensing question entirely.

### Sampling-station setup

- One trestle table (1.8m) for plated samples
- Greaseproof paper squares (~10×10cm) instead of plates — reduces washing, less waste than disposables, looks intentional
- Wooden picks for picking up sausage / strip (compostable)
- Small chalkboard or A6 card per cut: just the cut name + weight (e.g. "Sirloin / 25g" — no marketing copy)
- Bin for picks beside the table, labelled "compost"

## 4. Food safety — NZ Food Act 2014 compliance

**Status of the operation:**
- Mangaroa Farm Shop already operates under a Food Control Plan (FCP) for retail of meat. **Confirm with Carrie that the FCP covers on-site cooked sampling** — most retail FCPs do, but a one-line check with the verifier (or Hutt City Council EHO) is the safe move. If not covered, the event likely needs a National Programme 1 notification or a one-off event permit (~$0–$100, ~2 days lead time).
- Action: Carrie or Helena to ring the verifier or council EHO **by Tuesday 2026-05-06** to confirm. If a permit is needed, file by Wednesday.

**On-the-day controls:**
- Hand-wash station: jug + tap + soap + paper towels at the BBQ + at the sampling table. Refill water as needed.
- Cooked food held at >60°C or served immediately off the grill (preferred — cook in batches).
- Raw and cooked meat handled with separate tongs. Two sets of colour-coded tongs minimum (red for raw, blue/green for cooked is the convention).
- Thermal probe on the BBQ — beef strips internal >55°C medium-rare (rest 3 min), sausages >75°C, lamb shoulder >75°C if reheating.
- Hold-out time for any cooked sample sitting on the table: max 20 min. After that, into the bin or back on the grill briefly.
- Visible allergen signage: "Contains beef, lamb, gluten (in bread), and may contain traces of others. Ask Rob if unsure." A6 card at the sampling table.
- Sneeze guard or covered display dome over the sampling tray if bugs are around (Hōtoke risk: low).

**No tastings of raw or rare-rare cuts.** No charcuterie boards from Rob's experimental batches unless they're FCP-covered.

## 9. Contingencies

| Scenario | Plan |
|---|---|
| Rain forecast >70% | Move BBQ under the church porch or shop awning. Reduce sampling to plates from a covered station inside the shop. Cabinet stays the focus. |
| Wind sustained >40 km/h | Drop the gazebos (safety). Operate from the shop entrance with the BBQ as close to the wall as gas regs allow. |
| Cold snap (<8°C) | Bring out a brazier or fire pit if available. Hot drinks become the main draw. Shorten window to 11:30 – 13:30. |
| Cooking gas runs out | Spare 9kg bottle on standby. Identify nearest swap point (Mitre 10 Upper Hutt, BP) before the day. |
| Food running out before 14:00 | Switch to cabinet purchases as the experience. Rob does live demo cuts at the counter. Don't fake-stretch the BBQ. |
| Low turnout (<20 by noon) | Don't panic. Use the time to take Rob round the block knocking on neighbour doors with a tray. Capture content. The day still seeds bulk orders. |
| High turnout (>100 in first hour) | Slow the cook cycle so each person gets at least one taste. Pause sampling at 13:00 to top up the cabinet before serving the last batch. |
| Rob can't make it | Backup butcher / Murray runs the BBQ. Reframe as "meet the team" rather than "meet the butcher". Brand-voice rule: name the person who's actually there. |
| Cabinet stocks low end of day | Open the freezer cuts, put a "more in next week — bulk orders open" sign next to the cabinet. |
| Power failure | BBQ is gas — no impact. Coffee urn out. Use the moka pots / French press. Move on. |
| Food safety incident (someone unwell) | Stop serving immediately. Call Helena / Billy. Document time / cut / batch. Notify EHO within 24 hr per FCP requirements. |
