# Fresh Meat — Frequently Asked Questions

## What does "locally processed" mean?
Our beef and lamb are raised on Mangaroa's regenerative pastures right here in the valley. After professional slaughter, the carcasses come home to our local crew who handle all the butchery and processing. Shorter chain, more care, fresher meat.

## What cuts are available?
We carry standard retail cuts — steaks, mince, sausages, chops, roasts, and more. The range varies week to week depending on what has been processed. Drop in to see what is in the cabinet, or check online for current availability.

## Is the meat fresh or frozen?
We offer both. Fresh cuts are available in-store when they come through from processing. We also have frozen options for convenience and to reduce waste. Ask in the shop or check the product listings online.

## How do I order online?
Visit meat.mangaroa.org/order to browse what is available. Select your cuts, place your order, and pick up from the farm shop at a time that works for you.

## Can I order a whole lamb or side of beef?
Yes — we take expressions of interest for whole animals, half animals, and custom bulk orders. You can choose whether you want it butchered into standard cuts or collect the carcass to butcher yourself. Whole lamb starts from approximately $350-450 (butchered). Side of beef pricing varies by weight and is confirmed once we know the animal. Fill out the form at meat.mangaroa.org/bulk and we will be in touch to discuss timing and availability.

## When is fresh meat available in the shop?
Fresh meat is available in the farm shop during regular opening hours. Availability depends on the processing schedule, so the selection changes from week to week. Follow us on social media or sign up to our email list to stay in the loop.

## What animals do you have?
We currently offer beef and lamb. Both are raised on our regenerative pastures in Mangaroa Valley.

## What are your farming practices?
Mangaroa Farms practises regenerative agriculture — growing food in harmony with nature, not against it. We use innovative tools like virtual fencing to manage grazing, cover crops to build soil, and rotational systems that improve the land year on year. Our beef and lamb are pasture-raised on open paddocks with plenty of space and good feed. The same people who manage the land also handle the butchery — so the care runs from paddock to plate.

## Can I get meat shipped to me?
We can ship butchered orders within New Zealand. Unbutchered carcasses are local pickup only. Let us know your preference on the bulk order form and we will work out the best option.

## Where exactly does the meat come from?
Every cut comes from animals raised right here on Mangaroa Farms, 108 Whitemans Valley Road, Upper Hutt. You can visit the farm, see the paddocks, and meet the team. Rob manages the farm and the butchery — the same person who raises the animals prepares the cuts.

## How should I store fresh meat at home?
Keep fresh cuts refrigerated at or below 4°C and use within 3-5 days of pickup. For longer storage, freeze on the day you collect — well-wrapped meat keeps for several months in a home freezer. Mince and sausages are best used within 2 days fresh, or frozen straight away. Defrost slowly in the fridge, not on the bench.

## What if something I ordered online is not available?
We try to keep the online listings accurate, but the cabinet changes week to week. If a cut you ordered is not available when we go to fulfil it, we will be in touch — you can swap to something else, refund the difference, or hold the order for the next batch. Whatever works best for you.

## How far ahead should I order a whole lamb or side of beef?
Bulk orders depend on the processing schedule, so timing varies. As a rough guide, allow 2-4 weeks from the day you confirm to pickup. For a specific date (a freezer fill before winter, or a particular event), get in touch as early as you can and we will line it up.

## Are your sausages gluten-free? What is in them?
Our sausage recipes vary by batch. Ingredients are listed on the product label in the shop. If you have a specific allergy or dietary need, ask the staff or get in touch before ordering — we are happy to confirm what is in the batch you are buying.

## What if I have a problem with my order?
Get in touch as soon as you notice. We stand behind every cut that leaves the shop, and if something is not right we will sort it out — replacement, refund, or whatever fits. The shop number and email are on the contact page.
